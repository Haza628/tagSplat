Abstract

We propose a topology-aware dynamic reconstruction framework based on 3D Gaussian Splatting to address the challenge of consistent-topology mesh reconstruction and tracking in dynamic scenes. We design a Gaussian topology structure to encode spatial relationships among Gaussians, and introduce topology-preserving densification and pruning strategies to maintain manifold consistency. Starting from an initial mesh reconstruction, we initialize Gaussian parameters and enforce temporal consistency through 1-ring neighborhood regularization, enabling stable and coherent deformation across frames. A differentiable mesh rasterizer is incorporated to further enhance the quality of reconstructed meshes. The framework ultimately produces consistent-topology Gaussian and mesh sequences across frames, and supports accurate 3D keypoint tracking, offering a high-fidelity and low-cost solution for animation and related downstream tasks. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method outperforms existing approaches in both mesh reconstruction accuracy and tracking precision.
